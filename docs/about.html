What is this?

Lists of government ministers, and other key political leaders, from all over the world.

Why?

This sort of data is really important and useful in lots of scenarios, but is often surprisingly difficult to find. The CIA World Leaders list does the same sort of thing, but it has many problems, not least that it is usually very out of date (sometimes by years). Most other similar sources are less comprehensive, only publish very infrequently, or are only plain lists of names.

How often is the data updated?

Pretty much every day. I can't promise that everything is always completely up-to-date everywhere, but if something is out of date for more than a week, it's likely that I've missed an announcement somewhere, so please let me know.

A summary of updates each week is available via the Shuffled newsletter.

Where do you get the information from?

Most data comes from official government websites, or from news articles about new governments, cabinet reshuffles, etc. Wikipedia and Wikidata are also very helpful signals of changes, as in many countries these are updated quicker than official sources.
